College merchandise isn't just clothing — it's identity, pride, and memory. Whether it's your branch hoodie, fest t-shirt, hostel merch, or farewell sweatshirt, custom college apparel creates lasting bonds among students and becomes a tangible piece of your college experience that you cherish for years.

But getting college merchandise manufactured can be daunting — especially when you're dealing with tight budgets, diverse size requirements, complex designs, and manufacturers who don't take small orders seriously. This guide, built from our experience of delivering 75,000+ garments to IITs, NITs, and top institutions, walks you through every step of the process.

1 Why Custom College Merchandise Matters

College merchandise serves multiple purposes beyond just being wearable:

Identity & Pride

Custom merchandise with your college name, logo, branch, or hostel name creates a sense of belonging and pride. When 300 students wear matching hoodies at a tech fest, it creates an unforgettable visual impact and shared identity that strengthens community bonds.

Event Branding

Cultural fests, tech fests, sports meets, and hackathons all benefit from branded merchandise. Well-designed event t-shirts serve as marketing tools — every student wearing your fest shirt is a walking advertisement across campus and beyond.

Revenue Generation

Many college clubs and societies sell merchandise as a revenue stream to fund activities. A well-designed, well-made hoodie that students actually want to buy can generate significant funding for your club or fest. Premium quality means students are willing to pay ₹500–1,000+ per piece.

Lasting Memories

Years after graduation, that farewell hoodie or batch t-shirt becomes a nostalgic keepsake. Quality merchandise that survives years of wear becomes a treasured reminder of friendships, late-night study sessions, and campus life.

2 Types of College Merchandise You Can Order

Round-Neck T-Shirts (180 GSM Cotton)

The most popular choice for college events, fests, and batch merchandise. Affordable, comfortable, and available in 30+ colors. Perfect for screen-printed or DTF-printed designs with event logos, taglines, and artwork. Price range: ₹150–250 per piece for 100+ quantity.

Polo T-Shirts (180 GSM Cotton)

A more polished option ideal for department merchandise, placement drives, conferences, and club uniforms. Collar and button placket give a professional appearance. Often combined with embroidered logos for a premium feel. Price range: ₹250–400 per piece.

Hoodies (240 GSM Blend)

The crown jewel of college merchandise. Hoodies are the most cherished item — students wear them daily during winter months and long after graduation. Available with pullover or zip-up options, kangaroo pockets, and custom drawstring tips. Price range: ₹450–700 per piece. The premium feel of a quality hoodie makes students happy to pay ₹600+.

Sweatshirts (240 GSM Blend)

Similar to hoodies but without the hood — a cleaner, more minimal look that many students prefer. Great for branch merchandise, alumni merchandise, and everyday campus wear. Crew-neck or mock-neck options available. Price range: ₹400–600 per piece.

Caps & Accessories

Baseball caps, bucket hats, and beanies with embroidered college logos or event branding make great complementary merchandise. Often added as add-ons to t-shirt or hoodie orders. Price range: ₹100–250 per piece.

3 Step-by-Step Guide: From Design to Delivery

1

Form Your Merchandise Committee (Week 1)

Assign 3–5 dedicated committee members responsible for design coordination, size collection, budget management, and vendor communication. Having clear roles prevents miscommunication and delays. Appoint one person as the primary point of contact with the manufacturer.

2

Collect Size Data & Pre-Orders (Week 1–2)

Create a Google Form to collect sizes from all participants. Include options from XS to 3XL and ask for payment commitments. This data determines your exact order quantity and size breakdown. For accurate sizing, share a size chart from your manufacturer — Drift Minds provides detailed size charts for every product. Pro tip: Collect payment upfront (at least 50%) before placing the order to avoid last-minute cancellations.

3

Finalize Your Design (Week 2–3)

Create or commission your design. Use tools like Canva, Adobe Illustrator, or hire a designer. Provide vector files (AI, EPS, SVG) or high-resolution PNGs at 300+ DPI. Specify exact placement (front chest, back, sleeve), sizes of prints, and Pantone color codes. Run a design poll among students to choose the winning design — this builds engagement and reduces complaints.

4

Contact Manufacturers & Request Quotes (Week 3)

Reach out to 3–5 manufacturers with your requirements: garment type, fabric preference, quantity, design files, and delivery deadline. Compare quotes on: per-piece cost, fabric GSM, printing method, sampling cost, delivery timeline, and payment terms. Ask for references from other colleges they've worked with.

5

Approve Sample (Week 3–4)

Always order a physical sample before bulk production. Check fabric quality, print accuracy, color matching, stitching, and fit. Try the sample on someone of the appropriate size. At Drift Minds, sampling takes 7–15 days, and the sample cost is adjusted against your final order — so you essentially get the sample for free when you proceed with bulk.

6

Bulk Production & Quality Check (Week 4–6)

Once you approve the sample, production begins. Expect 2–3 weeks for bulk production depending on quantity. Request in-production photos at each stage. A quality-conscious manufacturer will share images of fabric cutting, printing, stitching, and final inspection. Payment is typically 50% advance + 50% before shipping.

7

Delivery & Distribution (Week 6–7)

Garments are packed size-wise and shipped via courier or logistics partners. Drift Minds ships pan-India with tracking. Once received, organize a distribution event on campus — this becomes a fun community moment. Pro tip: keep 2–3 extra pieces in popular sizes for exchanges or late additions.

4 Design Tips for College Merchandise

  • Keep It Clean & Bold: The best college merchandise designs are simple and impactful. A bold logo, clean typography, and 2–3 colors typically work better than overly complex illustrations. Your design needs to look good from a distance and in Instagram photos.
  • Include Key Information: College name/abbreviation, batch year, branch/department (if applicable), and event name. For farewell merchandise, include a memorable tagline or inside joke that resonates with your batch.
  • Consider Print Placement: Front chest logo (8–10 cm) + back design (A3 or A4 size) is the classic combination. Sleeve prints add a premium touch. For hoodies, consider a front print below the kangaroo pocket and a larger back design.
  • Use Your College Colors: Incorporating your college's official colors creates authenticity. Ask your manufacturer about Pantone matching to ensure exact color reproduction.
  • Run a Design Contest: Open design submissions to your batch or club, then run a poll. This creates ownership and engagement. The winning designer gets a free merchandise piece — a small incentive that generates great designs.

6 Budgeting & Cost Breakdown

Understanding the cost components helps you budget accurately and negotiate effectively:

Typical Cost Breakdown (Per Piece)

Fabric Cost 40–50% of total
Cutting & Stitching 15–20% of total
Printing / Embroidery 15–25% of total
Labels, Tags & Packaging 5–8% of total
Manufacturer Margin 10–15% of total
Shipping ₹30–80 per piece (pan-India)

Budget Tip: Add a 10–15% buffer to your collected amount for unexpected costs like design revisions, shipping charges, or exchange pieces. If you're selling merchandise, price it at 1.5x–2x your cost to cover expenses and generate a surplus for your club/committee.

7 MOQ Negotiation for Student Committees

Student committees often face the challenge of needing multiple products in smaller quantities. Here are strategies to maximize your negotiating power:

  • Combine Orders Across Departments: If your CSE department needs 100 hoodies and Mechanical needs 120, approach the manufacturer together as a single 220-piece order. Larger combined volumes get better per-piece pricing — typically 10–15% lower.
  • Standardize Base Colors: Using the same base color (e.g., black or navy) across multiple departments with different prints reduces the manufacturer's fabric sourcing complexity. Custom colors like maroon or forest green may require higher minimums.
  • Plan Multiple Orders Per Year: If your college orders fest t-shirts in August, branch hoodies in November, and farewell merchandise in April, commit to all three with one manufacturer. They'll offer loyalty pricing and faster turnaround for repeat customers.
  • Leverage Your Institution's Reputation: If you're from an IIT, NIT, or well-known institution, mention it. Manufacturers value institutional clients because they bring repeat business and credibility. At Drift Minds, we offer special pricing for premier institutions.
  • Ask About Student/Institutional Discounts: Many manufacturers, including Drift Minds, offer preferential pricing for educational institutions. Always ask — the worst they can say is no.
