Choosing the right fabric is arguably the most important decision you'll make when designing custom apparel. The fabric determines how a garment feels on the skin, how long it lasts, how well it takes prints, and how much it costs. Get it right, and your customers will love wearing your merchandise; get it wrong, and even the best design won't save a scratchy, ill-fitting t-shirt.

The two most common fabric choices for custom apparel — 100% cotton and polyester (or cotton-polyester blends) — each have distinct advantages and trade-offs. This guide provides an in-depth, data-driven comparison to help you make the right choice for your brand, event merchandise, or college apparel.

1 Understanding GSM – The Most Important Fabric Metric

GSM stands for Grams per Square Meter — it's the standard measurement of fabric weight and density. Think of it as the fabric's "thickness indicator." The higher the GSM, the heavier and generally more premium the fabric feels.

L

Lightweight

120–150 GSM

Ideal for summer wear, promotional tees, single-use event shirts. Thin and airy but may feel cheap. Transparent on lighter colors.

The sweet spot for premium t-shirts and polos. Good structure, comfortable drape, opaque, and excellent for all printing methods. Drift Minds' 180 GSM cotton sits perfectly here.

H

Heavyweight

Used for hoodies, sweatshirts, and winter wear. Thick, warm, and premium-feeling. Drift Minds' 240 GSM blend is ideal for hoodies and sweatshirts.

Pro Tip: Always ask your manufacturer for the exact GSM of the fabric they'll use. A "cotton t-shirt" could range from 120 GSM (tissue-thin promotional tee) to 220 GSM (premium streetwear quality). The price difference reflects the quality difference.

2 100% Cotton – The Classic Choice

Cotton remains the world's most popular apparel fabric, and for good reason. Grown naturally from the cotton plant, it has been used for clothing for over 7,000 years. India is the world's largest producer of cotton, making it abundantly available and competitively priced for domestic manufacturing.

Advantages of 100% Cotton

  • Superior Comfort & Breathability: Cotton's natural fiber structure allows excellent air circulation. It absorbs moisture from the skin, keeping the wearer cool and dry in hot weather. This is why cotton is overwhelmingly preferred for everyday wear in tropical climates like India.
  • Hypoallergenic: Cotton is naturally gentle on skin and rarely causes allergic reactions or irritation, making it suitable for people with sensitive skin. This is especially important for infant wear and undergarments.
  • Excellent Print Quality: Cotton's natural fiber accepts water-based inks exceptionally well, producing vibrant, detailed prints with screen printing and DTG methods. Colors appear rich and natural on cotton.
  • Biodegradable & Sustainable: Natural cotton decomposes within 1–5 months under the right conditions, unlike polyester which takes 20–200 years. Organic cotton takes this further with chemical-free cultivation.
  • Gets Softer with Washing: Unlike synthetic fabrics that can pill or harden over time, well-made cotton garments actually become softer and more comfortable with each wash.

Limitations of 100% Cotton

  • Prone to Shrinkage: Cotton can shrink 3–5% after the first wash if not pre-shrunk during manufacturing. This is why it's essential to use pre-shrunk or bio-washed cotton fabrics.
  • Wrinkles Easily: Cotton is naturally prone to creasing and requires ironing to maintain a crisp appearance. This can be a drawback for garments that need to look polished at events.
  • Slower Drying Time: Cotton absorbs and retains more moisture than synthetics, so it takes longer to dry after washing. This makes it less ideal for sportswear or high-intensity activities.
  • Color Fading: Over many washes, cotton colors can fade more quickly than polyester, especially darker shades. Reactive dyeing and proper wash care minimize this issue.

3 Polyester – Performance & Durability

Polyester is a synthetic fabric made from petroleum-based polymers. While it doesn't have the natural appeal of cotton, its engineered properties make it indispensable for certain applications — particularly sportswear, performance apparel, and sublimation printing.

Advantages of Polyester

  • Exceptional Durability: Polyester fibers are incredibly strong and resistant to stretching, shrinking, and abrasion. Polyester garments maintain their shape and color for significantly longer than cotton equivalents.
  • Moisture-Wicking: Polyester's hydrophobic properties mean it doesn't absorb moisture but channels sweat away from the skin to the fabric's surface where it evaporates. This makes it ideal for sports jerseys, gym wear, and performance apparel.
  • Wrinkle Resistant: Polyester garments resist creasing and maintain a smooth, fresh appearance without ironing — perfect for travel, uniforms, and professional settings.
  • Quick Drying: Polyester dries 3–4x faster than cotton, making it practical for outdoor activities, sports events, and workwear in humid climates.
  • Sublimation Printing: Polyester is the only fabric that supports dye-sublimation printing, which produces all-over, edge-to-edge, photo-quality prints that are permanently fused into the fiber. The colors are vibrant and virtually unfadeable.

Limitations of Polyester

  • Less Breathable: Polyester traps heat more than cotton, which can feel uncomfortable in hot, humid weather during extended wear without physical activity.
  • Static & Pilling: Polyester can develop static cling and small fabric balls (pilling) over time, particularly lower-quality variants.
  • Odor Retention: Polyester tends to trap body odor more than cotton because bacteria cling to synthetic fibers. This can be mitigated with antimicrobial treatments but remains a concern.
  • Environmental Impact: As a petroleum-derived product, polyester is not biodegradable and contributes to microplastic pollution during washing. However, recycled polyester (rPET) is increasingly available as a more sustainable option.

4 Cotton-Polyester Blends – Best of Both Worlds

Blended fabrics combine cotton and polyester fibers in specific ratios to capture the best properties of both materials while minimizing their weaknesses. The most common blends are:

60/40 CVC

60% Cotton, 40% Polyester

Cotton-dominant blend that retains the soft, breathable feel of cotton with added durability and reduced shrinkage from polyester. The most popular blend for premium casual t-shirts.

50/50 PC

50% Cotton, 50% Polyester

Equal blend offering balanced comfort and performance. Excellent wrinkle resistance with decent breathability. Widely used for corporate uniforms and bulk promotional merchandise.

65/35 TC

65% Polyester, 35% Cotton

Polyester-dominant blend emphasizing durability, wrinkle resistance, and quick-drying properties. Common in workwear, school uniforms, and institutional apparel where longevity matters most.

At Drift Minds, our 240 GSM poly-cotton blend is specifically engineered for hoodies and sweatshirts, providing the warmth and structure needed for winter-weight garments while maintaining the soft inner feel that cotton provides. This blend significantly reduces pilling and shrinkage compared to 100% cotton fleece, ensuring your merchandise looks premium even after dozens of washes.

6 Printing & Customization Compatibility

Your fabric choice directly impacts which printing methods you can use and how the final prints look and feel. Here's a breakdown:

Screen Printing

Best on: 100% Cotton > Cotton-Poly Blend > Polyester

Screen printing produces the most vibrant, long-lasting results on cotton. The natural fibers absorb ink deeply, creating a smooth finish. On polyester, plastisol inks can crack or peel, and dye migration (color bleeding from the fabric into the print) is a risk on dark polyester garments.

DTF (Direct-to-Film) Printing

Works well on: All fabrics equally

DTF printing is the most versatile method available today. It produces high-quality, full-color prints on cotton, polyester, and blends alike. The printed film is heat-pressed onto the fabric, making the base material less critical. This is Drift Minds' recommended method for complex, multi-color designs.

Embroidery

Best on: Cotton-Poly Blend > 100% Cotton > Polyester

Embroidery works on all fabrics but performs best on medium-to-heavyweight blends (200+ GSM) that provide stability. Very thin cotton or stretchy polyester can pucker during embroidery. Polo t-shirts and hoodies are ideal candidates for embroidered logos and crests.

Sublimation Printing

Requires: 100% Polyester (or 65%+ polyester blend, white/light colors only)

Sublimation produces stunning, photo-quality all-over prints but only works on polyester. The dye converts to gas under heat and bonds permanently with polyester fibers. Cotton and blends cannot achieve the same vivid results. This is the go-to method for sports jerseys and fully printed merchandise.

7 Best Use Cases for Each Fabric

Choose 100% Cotton For:

  • Everyday casual t-shirts
  • College fest merchandise
  • Brand merchandise for comfort-focused brands
  • Eco-conscious apparel lines
  • Summer wear and tropical climates
  • Screen-printed or DTF-printed designs
  • Premium D2C brand t-shirts

Choose Polyester For:

  • Sports jerseys & activewear
  • Marathon/event running tees
  • All-over sublimation printed apparel
  • Outdoor and adventure wear
  • Gym and workout clothing
  • Uniforms needing frequent washing
  • Quick-dry and moisture-wicking applications

Choose Blends For:

  • Hoodies and sweatshirts
  • Corporate uniforms
  • Polo t-shirts for professional wear
  • Winter/autumn casual wear
  • Merchandise needing extra durability
  • Apparel with embroidered logos
  • Budget-friendly bulk orders

Final Recommendation

There's no single "best" fabric — the right choice depends on your specific use case, climate, budget, and printing requirements. For everyday casual wear and college merchandise, 180 GSM cotton is the gold standard. For winter wear and hoodies, a 240 GSM cotton-polyester blend offers the best balance of comfort, warmth, and durability. For sportswear and performance applications, polyester excels.

Whatever fabric you choose, the key is working with a manufacturer who understands these nuances and can guide you to the right selection.
